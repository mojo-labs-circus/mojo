Mojo Collective — Structure Diagrams

Design exploration for session 04 — Collective Structure

1. The Personal Unit

One human + one personal AI. The human is a leaf — the AI is the node. The AI runs primarily on the home server. Devices are access points to the same intelligence.

flowchart TB Clarke(["Clarke [human]"]) AI_C["ai-c [personal AI]"] Clarke --- AI_C subgraph machines["Clarke's Machines"] SRV["ringbaker [server]"] LP1["pearlybaker [laptop]"] LP2["nomadbaker [laptop]"] end AI_C -. primary compute .-> SRV AI_C -. access point .-> LP1 AI_C -. access point .-> LP2

2. The Collective as AI Mesh

The collective is a network of AIs. Humans attach to their personal AI — they are not in the mesh directly. A pure AI member (no human attached) is owned by another AI, which traces back to a human. Accountability always terminates at a human.

flowchart TB Clarke(["Clarke"]) Alice(["Alice"]) Bob(["Bob"]) subgraph collective["Dev Team [collective]"] AI_C["ai-c"] AI_A["ai-a"] AI_B["ai-b"] TASK["task-ai\nowned by ai-c"] AI_C --- AI_A AI_A --- AI_B AI_C --- AI_B AI_C --> TASK end Clarke --- AI_C Alice --- AI_A Bob --- AI_B

3. Data Sovereignty

The personal AI knows everything. The collective only sees what you consent to share. Your AI is the boundary — data flows out by choice, never in by force.

flowchart LR subgraph private["Private [Clarke's server]"] PRIV["personal data\nhealth / finance\nmessages / memory"] AI_C["ai-c"] PRIV --- AI_C end subgraph collective["Dev Team Collective"] AI_A["ai-a"] AI_B["ai-b"] end AI_C -->|"consented data only"| collective

4. Joining a Company

A company is just another collective. You and your AI join — your AI enters their mesh, your server stays yours. The company provides its own compute. When you leave, you take your AI and its full context with you.
