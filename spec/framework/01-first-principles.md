# Session 01 — First Principles

> Foundation session. Every other session builds on the output of this one.
> Do not design anything here — only discover and establish.

---

## Purpose

Understand what collective intelligence is and why it exists — before designing
anything. The question is not "how do teams get things done." The question is:
why does intelligence seek other intelligence, and what emerges from that?

Singular intelligence in a machine is an established idea. This framework is an
attempt to model collective intelligence — a group of intelligences coordinating
toward a shared goal. To build it well, we first need to understand what it
actually is.

---

## The Core Questions

These are not design questions. They are questions about the nature of collective
intelligence itself.

**Why does intelligence collaborate?**

- What does an intelligence gain from other intelligences that it cannot have alone?
- Is collaboration a strategy, or something more fundamental — an emergent property
  of intelligence itself?
- Why do intelligent systems — human, animal, artificial — consistently form groups?

**What emerges from multiple intelligences working together?**

- What can a collective do that no individual member could, even in principle?
- What is synergy at the intelligence level — not just more hands, but something
  qualitatively different?
- When does collective intelligence fail to emerge, or collapse into less than
  the sum of its parts? What causes that?

**What is the interface between two intelligences?**

- How does one intelligence communicate with another? What is the minimum required?
- What is "shared context" — and why does it matter so much?
- What is trust between intelligences, and what builds or breaks it?

**Are there universal patterns?**

- Do the same patterns of collective intelligence appear across all substrates —
  human teams, animal collectives, neural networks, markets, ecosystems?
- If so, what are those patterns? Are they constraints imposed by physics and
  information theory, or something deeper?

---

## Research Directive

Survey collective intelligence across domains — not for productivity lessons,
but to find the underlying patterns.

**Natural systems:**

- Eusocial insects (ants, bees) — emergent coordination with no central controller
- Bird flocking and fish schooling — local rules producing global coherent behaviour
- Slime mould — distributed problem solving without a nervous system
- The brain itself — collective intelligence of neurons producing singular experience

**Human collectives:**

- Small hunter-gatherer bands — the original human collective intelligence
- Military units — collective intelligence under extreme constraint and pressure
- Jazz ensembles — structured freedom, emergent coordination, shared vocabulary
- Sports teams — role specialisation, real-time adaptation, collective flow states
- Scientific communities — distributed knowledge building across time
- Cities — unplanned collective intelligence at civilisational scale

**Organisational frameworks (as evidence, not as templates):**

- Military command doctrine — commander's intent, OODA loop, mission command
- Toyota Production System — how collective intelligence self-corrects and improves
- Dunbar's number and layers — why collective intelligence degrades above certain sizes
- Conway's Law — collective intelligence mirrors its own communication structure
- Amazon two-pizza teams — what happens when you constrain collective size

**Theoretical frameworks:**

- Swarm intelligence and stigmergy
- Extended mind thesis — does collective intelligence constitute a single mind?
- Information theory — what is the cost of coordination, and when does it exceed
  the benefit?

---

## Output Required

A set of named first principles — each one a claim about collective intelligence,
grounded in the evidence above. Not rules for teams. Statements about what
collective intelligence is and how it works.

Format each principle as:

- **Name** — one sentence statement
- **Evidence** — where this pattern appears across domains
- **Implication** — what this means for how we model collective intelligence

These principles are the axioms. Everything else in the framework is derived from them.
