# Aesthetic

## The Feel

A workshop, not a factory. Woody, not metal.

Warm and inviting — the kind of space that draws the light out of you. That makes you want to create something you're passionate about, not coerce you into doing what you feel like you have to. A place you sit down in and immediately want to make something.

The workspace should feel like somewhere you want to spend time — interesting, alive, a bit analog. Vintage technical instruments on a warm workbench. Not depressing, not sterile.

The reference points: aged paper, old NASA mission control, 1970s–80s technical instrumentation, vintage phosphor terminals. Warm retro-futurism — not cold cyberpunk.

Information-dense but not chaotic. Every element earns its place.

## Color Palette

### Surfaces (warm, aged, vintage)

| Role | Hex | Feel |
|---|---|---|
| Main background | `#F2ECD8` | Aged paper — the canvas everything sits on |
| Panel background | `#E8E0C8` | Slightly darker — cards, sidebars, containers |
| Panel header / border | `#DDD5B8` | Subtle separation without hard lines |

### Text (warm ink, not cold black)

| Role | Hex | Feel |
|---|---|---|
| Primary text | `#2A1F0E` | Very dark warm brown — old ink on paper |
| Secondary text | `#5A4A2E` | Lighter warm brown — labels, metadata |
| Muted text | `#8A7A5E` | De-emphasized — timestamps, hints |

### Accents (the energy)

| Role | Hex | Feel |
|---|---|---|
| Primary accent | `#C47820` | Amber — phosphor glow, brass instruments |
| Bright accent | `#E8960A` | Warmer amber — active states, highlights |
| Status green | `#6A9A5A` | Muted vintage green — alive, running, ok |
| Info blue | `#2A3A5A` | Dark navy — calm informational tone |

### Dark panels (terminals, code, data-dense views)

Dark surfaces are embedded within the warm canvas — like a CRT screen sitting on the workbench. They have their own internal palette:

| Role | Hex | Feel |
|---|---|---|
| Dark panel background | `#1C1A10` | Near-black with warm undertone — not pure black |
| Dark panel text | `#D4C89A` | Warm cream — readable, not harsh white |
| Dark panel accent | `#8AB870` | Muted phosphor green — data, status, output |
| Dark panel highlight | `#C47820` | Same amber — consistent energy color |

## Typography

- **Monospace everywhere** — data, labels, readouts, code, UI chrome. This is a technical instrument.
- Font candidates: `Berkeley Mono`, `Iosevka`, `JetBrains Mono` — something with character, not just functional.
- No rounded corners on text containers. Hard edges suit the aesthetic.
- Information hierarchy through weight and color, not font size sprawl.

---

## Layout Principles

- **Panel-based, not card-based** — panels have defined positions and purposes. Cards float. This doesn't float.
- **Hard edges** — minimal border-radius. Corners are corners.
- **Information-dense grids** — lean toward more data visible, not more whitespace. Trust the warm palette to keep it readable.
- **Spatial consistency** — things live in the same place every time. The agent knows where to surface things. The layout reinforces spatial memory.
- **Dark panels as focal points** — when something needs attention (a terminal, a data readout, a diagram) it lives in a dark panel. The contrast draws the eye without shouting.

---

## What It Should Feel Like

You open your workspace and it feels like sitting down at a warm, well-equipped workbench. Everything has a place. The tools are interesting to look at. The warm background makes long sessions comfortable. The amber and green accents feel alive — like instruments that are actually running, actually measuring something.

Not a productivity app. Not a chat interface. A workshop.
